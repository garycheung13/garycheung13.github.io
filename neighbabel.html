Project Overview

NEIGHBABEL

Problem Statement

In many of New York City’s residential neighborhoods, the makeup of the residential neighborhoods is extremely diverse in numerous ways. In the city alone, 200 different languages are spoken by New Yorkers. As a result, in individual New York City neighborhoods, one can hear multiple languages being spoken within a single location, none of which are English. These differences in language present a unique challenge to a neighborhood’s local businesses and organizations where they must find a solution to presenting and advertising their events and services in multiple languages. Currently, there are online services such as Meetup.com and Eventbrite where individuals and organizations can advertise their events. On a more local level, small organizations within a neighborhood promote events and services though placement of physical flyers and word of mouth. While the current strategies do the job of increasing awareness to an audience, it does not meet the needs of a multilingual population. Many online event planning sites have little or no participation from non-English speaking audiences and small organizations have few or no available resources to accommodate advertising in multiple languages.

Solution Overview

Neighbabel(working title) is a mobile application which decreases the barrier of community participation by giving organizations the ability ask, from the local area, for assistance on a short-term language related tasks. Additionally, organizations will be able to post for language volunteers, paid or pro-bono. These requests can range from minor language assistance the organization needs, such as translation for some of their advertising and announcement copy, to longer tasks such as assisting event participants in a particular language which the organization would otherwise not have the manpower to support. In order to ensure that businesses and community organizations get the best quality translation service, this app has been designed from a data-driven perspective. This is accomplished through the individual facing request feature of the app. Individual residents have ability to post a translation request for something they need during their days, such as asking for help speaking to someone who they don’t speak the language off, or help interpreting a document. Using these individual requests, the app can record metrics about how well the responding translator fulfills the request. Based on these metrics, the app can determine which translators would likely provide the best translator for an organization’s request.
