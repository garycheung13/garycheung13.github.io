Neighbabel – Find Local Language Help

An app designed to make finding local langauge help easier.

  • Duration

Project Summary

New York City is an extremely language-diverse city—over 200 languages are spoke here. Whilst there are services that are available in many of those languages, many others are only available in English. This can prove to be challenging for residents’ languages don’t include English. One way residents solve this problem is by asking for language help from friends, family, and close neighbors, but often times, this can be a very unclear process of searching for someone who both has the time and ability to help.

Design Solution

My app, tries to centralize the process of seeking help for language help. It allows users to post requests to look for users nearby who speak the same language. The app uses each user’s address book to check if there are common connections between the poster and those responding. This process enables to app to point out existing connection between two users. Through this app, I wanted to streamline the steps needed for NYC residents to seek language help from someone they know.

Features

Post Requests with the app

The app guides users through the process of making a request and asking for help.

View a familiar connection

Once someone responds to a request, the poster can check out how they already know the other person through mutual contacts.

Give a Rating

If the user has been helpful, Neighbabel lets the poster recommend that user to increase their reputation.

Design Process

Preliminary Research

The first step I took was performing a competitive analysis. I looked at several other organizations and websites who offer ways to connect people together and/or methods of offering language help.

Ideation – Sketching Ideas

I brainstormed and sketched out 8 different possibilities to solve the problem. For each solution, I further sketched out a 5 panel workflow for each to imagine the solution would work.

Interviewing Users and Creating Personas

I interviewed 4 different people who spoke English as second language, or had family who did, in-person and by phone, to find out more about different strategies used to solve language problems.

I learned several things from these interviews:

  • People preferred help from something they knew, they would trust this person’s words and advice more.
  • Language creates participation barriers in neighborhoods.
  • While interviewees who can family with interview needs were happy to help, it creates additional burden.

Making a Design Decision

Based on the interviews and personas, I made a decision to focus on an app which to connect users directly based on familiarity. I conducted a QOC (Questions, Options, Criteria) Analysis to determine the critical features my app needed to have.

Lo-fi Prototype and User Testing

I created a paper prototype containing the most important features of the app. Then asked classmates and users to try out the prototype to identify bad interactions, and created iterations to fix those problems.

Interactive Digital Prototype

After working on few iterations of the paper prototype, I build a high fidelity digital prototype in Sketch, and made it interactive with InVision.
