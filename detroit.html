Project Overview

Data Driven Detroit

Who is D3

Data Driven Detroit (D3) is a low-profit LLC whose aim is to provide data analysis services to public institutions in the southeast Michigan, with the focus on Detroit, to help public leaders make better, evidence driven decisions. D3 aims to cover the gamut of data analysis services for the public sector—they provide a wide range of data services including data collection, visualization, and interpretation. Many of their projects are initiated through partnerships with other local nonprofits who use D3’s data to advance their own missions. In addition to the D3’s nonprofit projects, they also aim to provide the greater Michigan community with better data and make much of their collected data openly available for public view and use.

The proposed problem

D3 proposed the question of how they they should should impove their the design of their internal database to our Contextual Inquiry class. I worked in a group with 3 other students to form a consulting group to begin helping them with the proposed problem.

Methodology

  • Contact: Our first action after being assigned Data driven Detroit was to make contact with our client and arrange an initial meeting at their office in Detroit. There, we became better acquainted with the proposed problem, scoped the depth of our project, and identified five potential employees to interview for our project.

  • Research: Our group held a meeting after the initial client meeting to decide on topics of interest for research related to our client’s industry sector. Each member of the group reviewed literature related to their topic and composed a 2,000-word report on it. Based on the collaborated information all of our reports, we constructed an interview protocol for each interviewee.

  • Interviews: Once our interview protocols were finalized, formed two-person teams to conduct each interview (based on time availability of both the interviewees and our group members). Individually, we reached out to each interviewee for an interview in their office. Each interview was to approximately 60-90 minutes long, and would include a period of work observation. At the end of each interview, our group got together to hold a meeting to interpret and discuss notes from our latest interview.

  • Afffinity Wall: (Shown below) After we finished conducting all five interviews, we processed our findings through a qualitative analysis process called an affinity wall. The affinity wall is a hierarchical diagram where each successive level looks at the problem from a higher-level perspective.

    To accomplish this task, we first broke down the notes from our interviews into individual statements and quotes, each of which went a separate yellow post-it. These yellow notes were grouped by topic and a blue note summarizing each group of yellow notes was posted above it. Once that phase was completed, we aggregated groups of blue notes together to draw a conclusion and created pink notes. Finally, on the highest level, we created green notes which summated the information on the pink notes on the highest level.

Outcomes

  • Key Finding:We determined through this process that D3’s challenges with designing their internal database structure were not, as were not technical, as initially accessed. We learned that D3 some internal communication issues have surfaced during the course of their startup’s growth, which has impeded progress on their database. The dedication analysts gave to external projects had caused communication with programmers working on internal projects to falter. While the construction of the database is an internal project, ultimately, the primary users will be the analysts working on external projects. Thusly, lack of communication created a major obstacle in development of a successful database since the needs of the users have not be considered with enough depth in the specifications of the database design.

  • Key Recommendation: In our final deliverable to D3, we included numerous recommendations, but the one primary recommendation we determined was to hold meetings between internal and external teams specifically discussing the progress of the database. D3 has already held meetings related to external projects to great success, and we believe that the same process can be implemented for their database project.

  • Outcome: After synthesizing recommendations, we created a 15-page consulting report documenting all of our methodologies, findings, and recommendations. In addition, we also gave a presentation on our findings and recommendations to leaders in D3. Our report was well received by the organization, and we were informed that several of our recommendations been given serious planning for implementation at the organization.
